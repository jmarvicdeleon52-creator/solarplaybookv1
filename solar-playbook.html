02 / 07 · Ad creative
01 / Ad creative

Three fixes that move CPL in AU solar.

Most solar ads fail at the same place: they lead with a panel price. The homeowner is shopping for a smaller power bill and the federal rebate before it shrinks. Match the ad to that, and the lead quality moves.

Fix 01 · Angle

Lead with bill savings, not the system price.

"Cut your power bill by 70%" beats "6.6kW system, $5,990" every time. Lead the headline with the savings story. Drop the dollar amount once they're listening, not before.

Fix 02 · Pricing frame

Quote in installments, not lump sums.

$7K upfront kills the click. $58/week absorbs into the budget like the bill they already pay. Reframe pricing as a weekly or monthly installment, ideally tied to the bill amount it replaces. "From $58/week, less than your current bill" outperforms a sticker price every time we test it.

Fix 03 · Imagery

Use real install photos. Skip the AI renders.

AI-generated solar imagery looks generic. Wonky panels, weird Australian-but-not-quite homes, suspicious shadows. Use actual photos from your installs. Phone-shot is fine if the install is clean. Authenticity beats polish in this niche.

Rule of thumb: your ad headline should describe the future. "Lower bill, locked-in rebate, panels next month." The price comes after the click.

03 / 07 · Targeting
02 / Targeting

Target like a local installer, not a generic media buyer.

Most generalist agencies stop at "homeowners 30-65, interested in renewable energy." That's lazy. Stack postcode, income, and STC zone deliberately, then let federal policy do the urgency work for you.

Tactic 01 · Zones

Postcode lists tied to STC zones.

Pull every postcode you actively service. Map each one to its STC zone (1 to 4). Build location targeting around postcode lists, not radius miles. This lets you write zone-specific ad copy. "Sydney homeowners, your 6.6kW rebate locks in by Dec 31" converts harder than a generic rebate line.

Tactic 02 · Audience stack

Income, age, and exclusions.

Layer top 25 to 50% household income, age 35 to 65, and exclude renters via interest stack. Solar is a 25-year asset. Younger homeowners often haven't decided to stay. Renters can't install at all. Filter both before they ever see your ad.

Tactic 03 · Year-end push

Use the STC drop as a real deadline.

Every January 1st, the rebate shrinks because the deeming period drops by one year. Run a December push that names the drop directly. "Your Zone 3 rebate drops on Jan 1, 2027" is a deadline you didn't have to invent. Federal policy does the work. Use it.

Note on STC zones: Zone 1 (top of NT, far north QLD) generates the most STCs per kW. Zone 2 (most of WA, central QLD). Zone 3 (NSW, VIC, SA, southern QLD, WA south, where most installs happen). Zone 4 (Tasmania, far southern VIC). Verify current deeming numbers with the Clean Energy Regulator before publishing specifics.

03 / Qualification

Five form questions that kill bad leads before your closer sees them.

Cheap leads are expensive when they don't close. The form is your first filter. Five questions, no more. Each one earns its place by removing a specific bad-fit prospect.

1. Are you the homeowner?

Yes/No. Renters never need to enter your CRM. Auto-route a "no" to a polite exit message.

2. What's your postcode?

Maps to STC zone for ad copy personalisation, and lets you confirm they're in your install radius before the call.

3. What's your average quarterly power bill?

Set a minimum (e.g. $400+ AUD per quarter). Below that, the savings math doesn't work. Don't waste closer time on $200-bill households.

4. What's your roof type?

Tile, tin, asphalt, flat. Some installers handle all, some don't. Filter for what your team actually installs and quotes confidently.

5. When are you looking to install?

Now / 1 to 3 months / 3 to 6 months / browsing. The "browsing" answer tags them for nurture, not a call. Closers focus on the first three.

Five short questions feel light to fill out. They remove tenants, bill-too-low households, out-of-area leads, wrong-roof leads, and not-ready browsers. That's a lot of waste filtered for a 30-second form.

05 / 07 · GHL CRM
04 / GHL CRM

A GoHighLevel pipeline you can build in an afternoon.

Five stages, three automations, one calendar. That's the whole CRM for a solo or small-team solar installer. Skip the 50-stage rabbit hole.

The pipeline

5 stages, in order.

01New enquiry. Fresh form submit, unqualified.
02Qualified. Form passed, contact verified by SMS or call.
03Sit booked. Appointment on the closer's calendar.
04Quoted. Closer sent a written quote, awaiting decision.
05Installed. Deal closed, panels on the roof.
The 3 automations that earn their keep

Speed-to-lead, no-show recovery, long-cycle nurture.

Speed-to-lead SMS, fired within 60 seconds: "Hey [first_name], thanks for the enquiry on solar for your home in [suburb]. I'm [closer_name] from [company]. Got 2 minutes to chat now or want me to call tomorrow morning?"

No-show recovery, fired 5 minutes after a missed appointment: "Looks like we missed you, no stress. Want to grab another time? [reschedule_link]"

Long-cycle nurture for "browsing" leads: a 5-touch sequence over 60 days. Soft education, no hard pitch. Keeps you front of mind for when the bill jumps and they're ready to act.

Before you launch: pixel and Meta Conversions API verified by the GHL form firing a real test event. Most installers skip this and lose 30% or more of their attribution. Don't be one of them.

05 / Launch

Seven days to launch. No fluff.

Most agencies take 60 days. You don't have 60 days. Here's the schedule that gets a properly built solar campaign live in a working week.

Day 1

Audit current setup

What's running, what's broken, what to keep. Pull last 90 days of Meta data and current GHL state.

Day 2

Build GHL pipeline and automations

5 stages, 3 automations, calendar wired. Test each automation with a fake submit end-to-end.

Day 3

Set up the lead form and verify pixel

5 qualifying questions live. Pixel and Conversions API confirmed firing on submit, not just on page load.

Day 4

Produce 3 creatives, 3 angles

Bill savings, installment pricing, year-end deadline. Static is fine. Real install photos beat polished video in this niche.

Day 5

Build the campaign

2 ad sets under one CBO budget. Audience: top 50% household income, age 35-65, postcode list, exclude renters. Each ad set runs a different offer angle head-to-head.

Day 6

Final QA

Form mapped to the right pipeline stage. Calendar showing closer availability. Speed-to-lead SMS firing. Pixel firing. Ad set toggled on.

Day 7

Launch

Go live in the morning. First lead within 24 hours if pixel learning is decent. Don't touch anything for 48 hours after launch. Let it learn.
